Blynk Controlled Robot and IoT based Automation

A smart way to control the devices and machines at home from anywhere.

The Fifth Revolution of this world has begun. This is the Internet of Things or the IoT. Our project focuses on the usage of the internet, a very powerful tool to control or drive robots,collect data and manage devices used in day to day life using open source platforms like Arduino and Blynk to create really useful pieces of machinery. I am using an Arduino Uno with a Motor Driver Shield and a Node MCU which is linked with the Blynk app to control the robot. In the other project we use an Arduino Uno, a relay and a Node MCU to control a 7 or 10 or even a 40 Watt LED or normal light.

  • Date: March 2017
  • Category: Robotics and Cloud Computing

WashPort

The Smart Portable Washing machine for Reusable Menstrual Pads

Use of Plastics has become indispensable due to its durability, anti-corrosive properties.Plastics take years to decompose and pose a major threat to our environment. The accumulation of plastic in our oceans is a greater cause of eco-system disruption. An estimated 100,000 marine animals die each year from suffocating on or ingesting bags.

The menstrual pads women normally use are 90% plastic and are harmful to the environment on disposal by burying or burning.An easy and ecofriendly alternative to this is using cotton cloth. Cotton cloth which is used can be very easily reused and has many advantages such as less toxic materials, less irritation and prevents other side effects of hard chemicals used in sanitary napkins.The Wash Port can be used as a machine to wash these cotton cloths isolated from other cloths in the house.Design focuses on a compact fully automatic machine which uses lesser water and consumes lesser power.

  • Date: 28th January 2018

Summer Programmes

The Duke Tip Summer Studies Programme 2015

I am an attendee of the Duke TIP 2015 Summer Studies Program. Duke University is a private research university located in Durham, North Carolina, United States. The Duke University conducts its Summer Studies program for school kids in India in May. This year (2015) it was held at Shiv Nadar University, Greater Noida.

In this amazing 20-day program, students from different places in India come to the SNU campus to stay, have fun and Study for 20-days. Students get to interact with top students of India and students of different backgrounds. This program includes 10 different and amazing courses which are

· Creative Writing: Express Yourself
· Cryptography, Codebreaking, and the Mathematics of Spying
· DNA: Unlocking the Genetic Code
· Engineering Problem Solving
· Entrepreneurial Leadership
· International Ingenuity: Political Simulations and Problem Solving
· Mathematical Problem Solving
· Modern Medicine: Disease, Immunology and Epidemiology
· Physics of Propulsion
&
· The Brain, Intelligence, and Creativity

I was selected for the Mathematical Problem Solving course. It was an amazing and cool course which in which we learnt the joy and awesomeness of math through the concepts of Game Theory, Paradox, Voting Theory, Probability, Fractals, Number Theory and Geometric Series. We learnt about unproven conjectures such as the travelling salesman problem and exciting proven theorems such as the 4 color theorem. We enjoyed making fun 3D fractals such as the Menger’s Sponge. We also had a wonderful time with the International Ingenuity guys, helping them to select the voting method they would like to use in the country they were going to make.

The residential area and the rooms were good .So we students had no problem. The residential counselors or RC’s were in charge of small groups of students. I think I got the best RC as he is a cool beatboxer (COOOOLLLL!!!!!!!!!!!!!!) , guitarist , DJ and a martial artist(sort of).Every day at campus in the evenings we had different activities such as sports, games, music, art etc. In the weekends we had DJ Nights where students could dance for music played .We also had residence time or free time when we could use the dorms and common area nearby to play or do activities with our friends. We had one Sports Day wher different teams namely The Red Dragons, The Blue Bandits, The Bribing Bananas and The Green Gargoyles battled against each other on the field for the prize. We played a lot of fun games and the winner emerged with highest number of wins.

Overall the Duke TIP 2015 was amazing and cool. I enjoyed the experience and I wish I could go there next year too.

  • Date: 30th May 2015
